A Rationale for This Electoral System & EVM

India's democracy is built on a "First-Past-The-Post" (FPTP) system, which was chosen for its simplicity. However, this system has critical flaws: it manufactures "false majorities," where a party can win 100% of the power with only 35-40% of the vote, and it forces citizens to vote "tactically" rather than for their true preference, "wasting" millions of votes in the process.

This mockup demonstrates a comprehensive electoral system designed to solve these problems by balancing national stability with voter fairness and, most importantly, by providing a modern, trustworthy, and verifiable voting process.

Part 1: The Proposed Electoral System (Lok Sabha)

This EVM is designed for a STV/List Parallel system. This system is superior to other reform proposals for three key reasons:

  • It's Fairer than "Bare" PR-STV: A system of "pure" STV (used for all seats) can be highly unfair. A party with 10% of the vote spread evenly across the country might fail to meet the quota anywhere and win zero seats. Our parallel system’s National List acts as a "safety net," ensuring that a party with broad national support gets the representation it deserves from the list seats.
  • It's More Stable than "MMP": The standard Mixed-Member Proportional (MMP) system, used in Germany, suffers from the "overhang seats" (Überhangmandate) problem. This is a constitutional nightmare where a party can win more local seats than it is entitled to, leading to a variable-sized parliament. Our parallel system has no such problem. The two sets of seats are counted separately, guaranteeing a fixed, predictable number of MPs.
  • It Prevents Fragmentation: To prevent the "khichdi" governments that the Constituent Assembly feared, the National List vote includes a 5% legal threshold. This is a powerful, proven tool that forces small parties to consolidate into larger, more stable national blocs to be eligible for list seats.

Part 2: A "No Black Box" Verifiable System

The single greatest challenge to any new EVM is the "black box" problem: how do you trust a result you cannot see? A complex STV count makes this worse. This entire system—from the EVM to the counting process—is designed to solve this trust problem.

The EVM: A Simple and Secure "Ballot Marker"

The EVM is not a "black box" but a "ballot marking device" designed to be "simple, secure and user-friendly".

  • Two-Part Ballot: The color-coded "Blue" (STV) and "Pink" (List) sections make the two-vote process intuitive.
  • Tactile and Robust: It avoids fragile touchscreens in favor of the current EVM's robust, button-based design.
  • The Officer Terminal: The live log (as seen in the mockup) is a secrecy-safe solution. It allows the polling officer to see that a voter is stuck (e.g., "Error: 'Next Preference' pressed without selection") but not who they are voting for, allowing them to provide technical help.

The Count: A Fast, Verifiable, and Auditable Process

This system rejects a full 100% manual STV recount, which is not just "tedious" but logistically impossible and, as the ECI itself has argued, "prone to human errors and... mischief".

Instead, it uses a modern, "trust but verify" model:

  • The EVM's Electronic Count (The First Resort): The EVM's electronic count provides the fast, provisional result on election night, which is a key ECI requirement.
  • The VVPAT as the "True Ballot": The VVPAT paper slip is the primary source of truth. As this mockup proposes, it would be printed with a machine-readable QR Code. This aligns with the Supreme Court's own 2024 suggestion for the ECI to explore using barcodes for machine counting.
  • The Audit (The "Gold Standard"): This is the final and most important step. We don't wait for "credible disputes." The ECI would proactively conduct a Mandatory Risk-Limiting Audit (RLA) as the final step of certification.
    • This is a public, statistical audit where a random sample of the QR-coded VVPAT paper slips is scanned.
    • This RLA can confirm, with 99.9% statistical confidence, that the electronic winner is correct without needing an impossible 100% manual count.

This model achieves what the Supreme Court and voters demand: a fast, electronic result that is proven correct by a modern, statistically ironclad, and transparent paper audit.

How This Voting System Works (Click to Expand)

You Are Casting Two Votes in One:

  1. A Local STV Vote (Section A): You rank your local candidates. This is used to elect the 5 representatives for your specific constituency. Some local candidates are allied with national parties, while others are local-only.
  2. A National List-PR Vote (Section B): You pick one national party. This is used to proportionally assign the 80 national seats in Parliament.

How Votes Are Counted:

  • Local STV (Ranking): A "quota" (minimum number of votes to win) is calculated.
    • If your #1 choice is elected: They only need a *part* of your vote's power. The *surplus* (extra) power from your vote is fractionally transferred to your #2 choice.
    • If your #1 choice is eliminated: The *full power* of your vote (at its current value) transfers to your #2 choice.
    This repeats until 5 winners are found. Your vote's power is never wasted!
  • National List (Party):
    1. 5% Threshold: First, any party that receives less than 5% of the total national vote is disqualified.
    2. Seat Allocation: The 80 seats are divided among the *remaining* parties based on their vote share.
    3. Rounding: All fractional seats are **rounded down** (e.g., 22.8 seats becomes 22).
    4. Surplus Seats: Any seats left over from rounding are allocated by random draw among the largest party (or parties, in case of a tie).

How to Use This EVM:

  1. After the officer presses the button, the Blue Section (Section A) will activate.
  2. Find your #1 preference and press the "Select" button next to their name.
  3. Press the large "Next Preference" (Green) button to move on.
  4. Find your #2 preference and press "Select".
  5. Repeat this for as many candidates as you wish to rank. Use the "Clear Last Choice" (Yellow) button if you make a mistake.
  6. When you are finished ranking (even if it's just one), press the "Confirm Rankings" (Red) button.
  7. The Pink Section (Section B) will now activate.
  8. Select your one choice for the national party by pressing the blue "VOTE" button.
  9. A final "BEEP" will sound. Your vote is cast.
  10. Verify your choices on the VVPAT slip that appears.

Note for Polling Officers:

When you activate the EVM, a "Polling Officer Terminal" will appear on your screen. This terminal logs the voter's actions (e.g., "Voter selected Preference #1", "Voter confirmed rankings") and any errors (e.g., "Voter pressed 'Next' without selection"). This allows you to provide verbal guidance to a voter who seems stuck, without compromising the secrecy of their ballot. The log resets for every voter.

How to Cast Your Vote: A Simple Guide

1. Listen for the BEEP

After the polling officer activates the machine, you will hear a beep. The BLUE section (PART A) is now active.

PART A: Your Local (STV) Vote

2. Find Your #1 Choice

Find your #1 preferred candidate. Press the "Select" button next to their name.

3. Press "Next Preference"

Press the large GREEN "Next Preference" button to move to your next rank.

4. Find Your #2 Choice

Find your #2 preferred candidate. Press the "Select" button next to their name.

5. Repeat as Desired

You can rank as many candidates as you like by repeating steps 3 and 4.
Mistake? Press the YELLOW "Clear Last Choice" button to erase your last selection.

6. Confirm Your Rankings

When you are completely finished ranking, press the large RED "Confirm Rankings & Proceed" button. This locks in your local vote.

PART B: Your National (List) Vote

7. Select Your National Party

The PINK section is now active. Find your ONE preferred national party. Press the BLUE "VOTE" button next to its name.

8. Listen for the FINAL BEEP

A long, final "BEEP" will sound. Your vote is now successfully cast.

9. Check the VVPAT

Look at the glass window on the VVPAT printer. A slip will appear for 7 seconds. Verify that BOTH your STV ranks and your National Party choice are correct.
